Decline curves: Production and Injection potentials

 

 

 

 

User can access the DCA module by clicking on the "Decline Curves" icon from Precept main User Interface.

 

The declines Curves Analysis (DCA) module is a powerful module from which Users can define individual Well Potentials. During forecast, shutdown and constraints are applied to Well potentials to generate constrained forecast (the difference being shortfalls).

 

 

At opening, User needs to select the Project and Scenario and the Well that requires DCA generation.

Analogue wells can be plotted in the background to be used as guidelines.

 

 

5 types of DCA methods can be used to generate Well Potentials:

 - ARPS exponential

 - ARPS Hyperbolic

 - ARPS Harmonic

 - "Multi-segment vs Cum"

 - "Multi-segment vs Time"

Multi-segment vs Cum

In the "Multi-segment vs Cum" method:

 - for Oil producer, Well potential is generated by modifying Liquid rate, BSW and GOR. Within one segment, Liquid rate, BSW and GOR are a modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Oil

 - for Gas producer, Well potential is generated by modifying Gas rate, Water-Gas-Ratio (WGR) and Condensate-Gas-Ratio (CGR).  Within one segment, Gas rate, Water-Gas-Ratio (WGR) and Condensate-Gas-Ratio (CGR)are a modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Gas

 - for Water Injector, Well potential is generated by modifying Water Injection rate. Within one segment, Water Injection rate is modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Water Injection.

 - for Gas Injector, Well potential is generated by modifying Gas Injection rate. Within one segment, Gas Injection rate is modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Gas Injection.

In the "Multi-segment vs Time" method:

 - for Oil producer, Well potential is generated by modifying Liquid rate, BSW and GOR. Within one segment, Liquid rate, BSW and GOR are a modeled as a linear function of Time

 - for Gas producer, Well potential is generated by modifying Gas rate, Water-Gas-Ratio (WGR) and Condensate-Gas-Ratio (CGR).  Within one segment, Gas rate, Water-Gas-Ratio (WGR) and Condensate-Gas-Ratio (CGR)are a modeled as a linear function of Time

 - for Water Injector, Well potential is generated by modifying Water Injection rate. Within one segment, Water Injection rate is modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Water Injection.

 - for Gas Injector, Well potential is generated by modifying Gas Injection rate. Within one segment, Gas Injection rate is modeled as a linear function of Cumulative Gas Injection.

 

 

Auto-forecast using ARPS on Oil, Liquid and Gas

The below steps can be followed to generate Low / Mid and High cases for an Oil Producer

 - Select the well to be forecasted

 - Select Mid case

 - Select "Hyperbolic" as method of DCA

 - Select the historical production data points representative of the well performance

 - Unselect the historical production that may not seem relevant to well performance or outliers

 - In "Settings", indicates the remaining forecast period in Years, and the reserves ratio to be used to generate Low and High cases.

 

 

 - Click on Auto-forecast

 - Adjust Mid case manually if needed

 - Click on "Auto-forecast Low and High from Mid".

 - In settings, check the "Probabilistic" option to visualize P10, P20,...P90 that will be used during Probabilistic forecast.

 

 

 

 

 

Auto-forecast using ARPS on Liquid, straight line on BSW and GOR vs Cum

The below steps can be followed to generate Low / Mid and High cases for an Oil Producer

 - Select the well to be forecasted

 - Select Mid case

 - Select "Multi-segment vs cum" as method of DCA

 - Select the historical production (Liquid, BSW, GOR) data points representative of the well performance

 - Unselect the historical production that may not seem relevant to well performance or outliers

 - In "Settings", indicates the remaining forecast period in Years, and the reserves ratio to be used to generate Low and High cases.

 

 

 - Click on Auto-forecast.

The Liquid is forecast is forecast using ARPS.

BSW is forecasted using Linear relationship between BSW and Cum Oil.

GOR is forecasted using Linear relationship between GOR and Cum Oil.

 

 - Adjust Mid case manually if needed

 - Click on "Auto-forecast Low and High from Mid".

 

 

 

 

Modify Decline Curves Manually

Modifying decline curves can be performed using 2 methods:

 - change manually the value in the Potential table

 - drag and drop the "dots' on the chart (for "Multi-segment vs Time" and "Multi-segment Vs Cum", segment can be added or removed using the + and - button)

 

 

 

Lock X-Axis

Right-click on a DCA charts to activate the "X-Axis locked" mode. That way, when modifying DCA using the mouse, points will not move on the X_Axis.

 

 

Allow Zoom

Double-click on a DCA charts to allow zooming. Double-click again to disallow zooming.

 

 

Low, Mid and High Case

For every well, Low, Mid and High case can be generated. The Low, Mid and High Case are used when generating probabilistic forecast.

 

 

Plot DCA.

When the drop-down menu is set to Plot DCA, user can plot several parameters (oil, gas, water, BSW, GOR etc ) vs Cum, Date or Production time.

It is not possible to modify DCA by drag and drop on the "plot DCA" charts. "Plot DCA" charts are for vizualization only.
